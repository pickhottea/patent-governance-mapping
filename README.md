# Patent → Data & AI Governance Mapping

**Translating patent engineering work into data & AI governance capabilities: lifecycle management, risk analysis, auditability, compliance, and third-party coordination.** 

Patent engineering is often misunderstood as purely legal or administrative work. In practice, my role involved structured monitoring, risk-oriented analysis, documentation, portfolio maintenance, external coordination, and decision support in a regulated technical environment.

My professional experience included:
- monthly and topic-specific patent searches based on defined search profiles
- analysis and assessment of documents regarding development, threat, and infringement potential
- Freedom-to-Operate and third-party rights searches
- monitoring of third-party intellectual property rights
- patent-law documentation of development activities for evidence preservation
- preparation of documents for patent and trademark applications
- support with patent examination procedures
- close cooperation with external patent and trademark attorneys
- evaluation and maintenance of patent and trademark portfolios

This mapping shows how these activities translate into governance-relevant capabilities such as lifecycle management, risk identification, controlled workflows, audit-ready documentation, traceability, and third-party governance.

## Governance Capability Map

| Patent Engineering Experience | Governance-Relevant Capability |
|---|---|
| Portfolio maintenance of patents and trademarks, including legal status, applications, and strategic fields | **Data Lifecycle & Ownership** |
